# Educational Philosophy

This is the foundational document for Epiphany. Every system choice — from chart layout to review copy — should be defensible against these principles.

---

## Acquisition Over Memorization

Epiphany prioritizes **language acquisition**: the learner gradually perceives Greek as understandable, patterned, and speakable.

We avoid:

- Isolated flashcard grinding without context.
- Grammar rules presented before the learner has felt the pattern.
- Trophy mechanics that reward clicks over comprehension.

We pursue:

- **Comprehensible input** in readings and dialogues.
- **Pattern recognition** before formal terminology (aspect matrix Intuition mode before Grammar mode).
- **Chunk learning** — θέλω να…, μου αρέσει…, λοιπόν… — as whole usable units.

Understanding is the product. Recall is evidence of understanding, not the goal itself.

---

## Greek Thinks Differently (Respect the Language)

Modern Greek pedagogy here is built around **how Greek actually organizes meaning**:

- **Quantity of action** matters as much as clock time (ongoing vs one-time vs already done).
- Verb forms often **build in layers** (present stem → past ongoing → past complete → έχω + participle).
- Sound changes (παίζω → παίξω) are **systematic**, not random exceptions to fear.

Lessons like *How Greek Thinks About Time* are flagship because they give learners an **emotional and logical** hook — not a table to memorize.

Linguistic insight boxes (“Why Greeks say it this way”) connect form to intuition. They are not trivia — they are **affective bridges** to difficult concepts.

---

## Immersion Through Density, Not Overwhelm

Immersion does not mean dumping advanced text on beginners. It means:

- The learner is **surrounded by understandable Greek** at their level.
- Previously learned patterns **reappear naturally** in readings, dialogues, and practice.
- Conversation glue (λοιπόν, μάλλον, εντάξει) appears early so Greek sounds **human**.

Reading tiers (1–4) and unlock gates ensure density increases **intentionally**, not accidentally.

---

## Contextual Repetition

Repetition is essential; **decontextualized** repetition is not.

Epiphany recycles grammar through:

- Stories that reuse tense contrasts after lessons introduce them.
- Dictation and shadowing drawn from dialogue lines the learner has seen.
- Weak-spot drills tagged to **pattern tags** (e.g. `verb-present`, `article-feminine`), not random words.
- Review Today surfacing **why** something needs attention.

The learner should feel: “I’ve seen this before — now I understand it better,” not “Why am I drilling this again?”

---

## Calm, ADHD-Friendly Design

Many learners (including ADHD profiles) need:

- **Clear next steps** — recommended lesson, guided paths, Review Today.
- **Short wins** — micro-dialogues, mastery level chunks, readable session lengths.
- **Low shame** — near-miss recognition, gentle confidence messages, no public leaderboards.
- **Focus modes** — Study Focus for charts; minimal chrome; escape always available.
- **Sensory control** — speech rate, dark mode, optional pronunciation display.

Avoid: flashing rewards, punitive streak loss, cluttered dashboards, unexplained locks.

The app should feel like a **patient teacher**, not a slot machine.

---

## Pattern Recognition Before Rules

Instructional sequence (idealized):

1. **See** the pattern (chart, dialogue, highlight).
2. **Feel** the contrast (aspect matrix, transformation pairs).
3. **Use** the pattern (typing, sentence builder).
4. **Name** the pattern (grammar labels, reference charts).

Formal grammar is always available — but rarely the **first** exposure.

---

## Connected Curriculum

No lesson is an island. The intended loop:

```
Learn concept → Dialogue exemplifies → Reading reinforces →
Practice drills → Review resurfaces later → Advanced reading recombines
```

Guided paths and unlock requirements encode this loop explicitly. Content authors should ask: *where does this concept appear next?*

---

## Confidence Without Gamification

Feedback should describe **growing competence**:

- “You are naturally recognizing Greek action patterns.”
- “You now understand repeated past actions.”

Not:

- “+50 XP”
- “Level 7 Warrior”

Streaks exist as **habit nudges**, not identity status.

---

## Implications for Future Systems

Any new feature should pass:

1. Does it increase **understandable Greek exposure**?
2. Does it **reuse** existing primitives (vocab, stories, patterns)?
3. Does it feel **calm** and **intentional**?
4. Would a creator understand **where it fits** in the curriculum loop?

If not, reconsider or redesign.
